How Part D Formularies Work

What is a formulary?

The list of drugs your Medicare Part D or Medicare Advantage plan covers, organized into pricing tiers.

What are drug tiers?

Tiers group covered drugs by cost — Tier 1–2 are low-cost generics, Tier 3 preferred brands, Tier 4 non-preferred, Tier 5 specialty drugs.

What is prior authorization?

Your plan must approve the drug before covering it, usually to confirm it's medically necessary.

What is step therapy?

You may need to try one or more lower-cost drugs first before the plan covers a more expensive option.

What are quantity limits?

Plans may limit how much of a drug they cover over a period of time for safety or cost reasons.

What if my drug is not covered?

You can request a formulary exception and appeal, switch to a covered alternative, or compare plans during enrollment. We can help.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Medicare cover Ozempic?
Part D plans typically cover Ozempic for type 2 diabetes — usually on a higher brand tier with prior authorization. It's generally not covered for weight loss alone.
What is a formulary?
The list of drugs your Part D or Medicare Advantage plan covers, organized into pricing tiers.
What are drug tiers?
Tiers group covered drugs by cost: lower tiers are generics with low copays; higher tiers are preferred brands, non-preferred brands, and specialty drugs.
What is prior authorization?
Your plan must approve a drug before covering it, usually to confirm it's medically necessary.
What is step therapy?
You must try one or more lower-cost drugs first before the plan covers a more expensive option.
Can I appeal a coverage denial?
Yes — you can request an exception and file an appeal. Your doctor or a licensed agent can help with the paperwork.
