Understanding Your Enrollment Periods

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP)

A 7-month window around your 65th birthday — the 3 months before your birthday month, your birthday month, and the 3 months after. This is when most people first sign up for Medicare.

Special Enrollment Period (SEP)

If you have active employer coverage from a company with 20+ employees past 65, you can delay Part B and enroll later — usually within 8 months of that coverage ending — without a penalty.

Annual Enrollment Period (AEP)

October 15 – December 7 each year. You can join, switch, or drop a Medicare Advantage or Part D plan for the following year.

General Enrollment Period (GEP)

January 1 – March 31 each year, for people who missed their Initial Enrollment Period. Late-enrollment penalties may apply.

What happens if I delay Medicare?

Without qualifying employer coverage, delaying Part B or Part D can add a lifetime late-enrollment penalty to your premiums — and you may have to wait for the General Enrollment Period to sign up.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who qualifies for Medicare before age 65?
People who have received Social Security disability benefits for 24 months, or who have End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) or ALS, can qualify before age 65.
When does Medicare start?
If you enroll during the 3 months before your 65th birthday, Medicare generally starts the first day of your birthday month. Enrolling later can delay your start date.
When should I enroll?
Most people enroll during their Initial Enrollment Period — the 7-month window that begins 3 months before the month you turn 65 and ends 3 months after.
What if I keep working after 65?
If you have active employer coverage from a company with 20 or more employees, you may be able to delay Part B without penalty and use a Special Enrollment Period when that coverage ends.
What is the Annual Enrollment Period?
From October 15 to December 7 each year, you can join, switch, or drop a Medicare Advantage or Part D plan for the following year.
What happens if I miss my Initial Enrollment Period?
You may need to wait for the General Enrollment Period (January 1 to March 31) and could face lifetime late-enrollment penalties for Part B and Part D.
