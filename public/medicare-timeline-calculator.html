Frequently Asked Questions

When should I enroll in Medicare?
Most people enroll during their Initial Enrollment Period — the 7-month window that begins 3 months before the month you turn 65 and ends 3 months after.
What is the Initial Enrollment Period?
A 7-month window around your 65th birthday: the 3 months before your birthday month, your birthday month, and the 3 months after.
Can I delay Part B?
Yes, if you have active employer coverage from a company with 20 or more employees. Without qualifying coverage, delaying can trigger a lifetime late-enrollment penalty.
What is a Special Enrollment Period?
It lets you enroll outside your Initial Enrollment Period without a penalty — usually within 8 months of losing active employer coverage.
What happens if I miss my enrollment window?
You may need to wait for the General Enrollment Period (January 1 to March 31) and could face lifetime late-enrollment penalties for Part B and Part D.
